Oil & Fluid Maintenance

Even modern cars need regular attention—especially their six main fluids. Proper fluid levels and quality are essential for performance, longevity, and safety.

  • Engine Oil: Keeps engine components moving smoothly. Check level and condition regularly; change as needed.
  • Coolant: Absorbs and dissipates engine heat. Prevents overheating; check at least every 50,000 miles.
  • Power Steering Fluid: Hydraulic systems rely on this for easy steering. Check regularly.
  • Brake Fluid: Can become contaminated by water, causing spongy pedals or corrosion. Inspect and flush as needed.
  • Transmission Fluid: Lubricates and cools transmission components. Check if you notice rough shifts or strange noises.
  • Windshield Washer Fluid: Essential for visibility and safe driving. Top off whenever possible.

Tip: Regular checks and maintenance prevent costly repairs and keep your car running safely and efficiently.

Tires (general)

As tires wear, their performance decreases—especially on wet, snowy, or icy roads. AV Repair can measure your tire tread depth to determine if they are safe for the road or need replacement.

For example, a car traveling at 70 mph with tires at the legal minimum tread depth of 2/32" can take about 100 feet longer to stop compared to tires with 4/32" tread depth. At that point, the vehicle with worn tires may still be moving at 45 mph while the better-treaded car has stopped.

Replacing tires before they reach the legal minimum may seem costly, but it’s far safer and less expensive than repairing damage from an accident due to poor stopping performance.

Tire Rotation & Balancing

Rotating and balancing your tires is the most efficient way to extend their lifespan. Tires wear unevenly due to friction, much like shoes, and replacing them can be costly.

Rotation involves moving tires according to manufacturer specifications, while balancing uses a machine to check the weight distribution of the tire and wheel. Small lead weights are added if necessary to ensure even balance.

Most manufacturers recommend rotating and balancing all four tires at least every 7,000 miles. A convenient approach is to do this each time you have your oil changed.

Proper rotation and balancing improves safety by maintaining even tread wear—especially on front tires, which wear faster and affect vehicle control—and efficiency by allowing all four tires to wear evenly, reducing the need for premature replacement.

Regular rotation and balancing helps prevent slipping or skidding and maximizes the lifespan of your tires.

Brake Repair

Average Price: Sedan: $250–$350 per axle, SUV: $350–$450 per axle. Call for an estimate for your specific vehicle.

At AV Repair, we prioritize your safety and comfort. Here are common brake issues and the services we provide:

  • Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS): If your ABS light comes on, we can diagnose and repair the problem.
  • Brake Fluid Flushes: Over time, brake fluid absorbs water, reducing effectiveness and causing corrosion. Periodic flushes keep your system safe.
  • Brake Hoses: Cracked or leaking hoses can cause slow braking or total brake failure. We replace worn hoses promptly.
  • Brake Pad & Shoe Replacement: Squealing brakes indicate wear. Ignoring worn pads can damage rotors or drums. Get your brakes inspected immediately if you notice symptoms.
  • Caliper Replacement: Calipers clamp the pads to the rotor. Faulty calipers can cause uneven braking, making your car slide, especially in bad weather.
